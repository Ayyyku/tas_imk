History

The Sailendra dynasty built the largest Buddhist relic in the world between 780-840 AD. The Sailendra dynasty was the ruling dynasty at that time. This relic was built as a place of worship for Buddhists and a place of pilgrimage. This place contains instructions for humans to distance themselves from worldly desires and move towards enlightenment and wisdom according to Buddha. This relic was discovered by British troops in 1814 under the leadership of Sir Thomas Stanford Raffles. The temple area was completely cleared in 1835.
Borobudur was built in a Mandala style which reflects the universe in Buddhist belief. The structure of this building is box-shaped with four entrances and a circular center point. When viewed from outside to inside, it is divided into two parts, namely nature which is divided into three zones on the outside, and the realm of Nirvana in the middle.
Zone 1:Kamadhatu
nature that humans see and experience today. Kamadhatu consists of 160 reliefs explaining the Karmawibhangga Sutra, namely the law of cause and effect. Describes human nature and desires, such as robbing, killing, raping, torturing, and slandering. The hood at the base has been permanently opened so that visitors can see the reliefs hidden at the bottom. A photo collection of all 160 relief photos can be seen at the Borobudur Temple Museum in the Borobudur Archaeological Park.
Zone 2: Rupadhatu
is a transitional area, where humans are free from world affairs. Rapadhatu consists of a gallery of carved stone reliefs and Buddha statues. In total there are 328 Buddha statues which also have relief decoration on their carvings. According to Sanskrit manuscripts, this section consists of 1300 reliefs in the form of Gandhawyuha, Lalitawistara, Jataka and Awadana. The whole thing stretches for 2.5 km with 1,212 panels.
Zone 3: Arupadhatu
the highest realm, the house of God. Three circular porticoes lead to a central dome or stupa depicting the awakening of the world. In this section there are no decorations or ornaments which means it depicts the highest purity. The porch in this section consists of a circular stupa with a hole, an inverted bell, containing a Buddha statue leading to the outside of the temple. In total there are 72 stupas. The largest stupa in the center is not as tall as the original version, namely 42m high above ground level with a diameter of 9.9m. Unlike the stupas that surround it, the central stupa is empty and has given rise to debate that it actually contains contents, but there are also those who argue that the stupa is indeed empty.
